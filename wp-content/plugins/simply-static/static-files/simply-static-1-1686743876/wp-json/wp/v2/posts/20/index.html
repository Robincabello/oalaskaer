Not since the 1970s has Steve Perillo, CEO and third-generation owner of New Jersey-based tour operator\u00a0Perillo Tours, seen such a spike in travelers booking trips to Europe.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The company, which is best known for its custom tours to Italy, usually sells about 80% of its capacity in a year, Perillo says. So far in 2023, approximately 96% of the operator\u2019s 500 annual departures, which also include Spain and Greece as destinations, are booked \u2013 and Perillo expects that figure to hit 100% within a few weeks, before the company starts marketing for its 2024 season.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

For Perillo, hints of the blockbuster season ahead started appearing last fall, when the company offered a Black Friday discount of about 5%. \u201cWe should have charged more for Black Friday,\u201d he jokes. \u201cThat\u2019s when we noticed that [demand] was outrageous, and I called all my friends the night of Black Friday and said, \u2018this is going to be crazy.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In many parts of Europe, the craziness is already well underway, as droves of Europe-bound tourists follow in the well-trodden footsteps of their 2022 predecessors in last season\u2019s post-Covid lockdown surge.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to travel insurance provider Allianz Partners, the number of Americans traveling to Europe this summer is expected to soar by\u00a055% over last year\u2019s figures\u00a0\u2013 which were already a staggering 600% higher than in 2021. London, Paris, and Dublin topped the 2023 list of the 10 most popular destinations revealed by Allianz\u2019s annual review; Rome, Reykjavik, Amsterdam, and Lisbon also made the top 10.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The European Travel Commission also forecasted\u00a0strong demand for European vacations in 2023, with nearly half of all destinations on the continent expecting a return to more than 80% of 2019 numbers.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
